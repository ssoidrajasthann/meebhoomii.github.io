MeeBhoomi

MeeBhoomi (translating to "Your Land") is the official digital land records repository launched by the Government of Andhra Pradesh in June 2015. It serves as a transparent interface for citizens to access critical documents like the Adangal and Record of Rights (1-B) without visiting the Tahsildar's office.

The portal digitizes land data across all 26 districts of Andhra Pradesh, allowing landowners to verify ownership details, check crop data, and even link their Aadhaar numbers to their survey numbers for added security.

Understanding Key MeeBhoomi Documents

1. What is Adangal (Pahani)?

The Adangal (also known as Pahani in other states) is the primary land record maintained by the Village Revenue Officer. It contains:

  • Owner Details: Name of the landholder.
  • Land Type: Classification of soil and land nature (Wet/Dry).
  • Cultivation: Crops grown, water sources, and yield estimation.
  • Liabilities: Details of existing loans or mortgages on the land.

2. What is ROR 1-B?

The Record of Rights (1-B) is the legal proof of land ownership. Unlike the Adangal which focuses on cultivation, the 1-B is strictly an ownership title record used for:

  • Obtaining bank loans.
  • Property registration and sales.
  • Legal disputes in court.

3. Field Measurement Book (FMB)

The FMB contains the sketch of land boundaries as per official survey records. It is crucial for resolving boundary disputes between neighbors. The MeeBhoomi portal allows you to download these sketches digitally.

How to Download Your Adangal Online

Follow these steps to view your land details on the official portal:

  1. Visit the Portal: Go to meebhoomi.ap.gov.in.
  2. Select 'Adangal': In the menu, choose "Your Adangal" (Personal) or "Village Adangal" (Public).
  3. Enter Location Details: Select your District, Mandal, and Village from the dropdowns.
  4. Search Method: You can search using:
    • Survey Number
    • Account (Khata) Number
    • Aadhaar Number
    • Pattadar Name
  5. Verify & Submit: Enter the Captcha code and click "Click" to generate the report.

How to Link Aadhaar with Land Records

Linking Aadhaar prevents fraudulent transactions. To check your status:

  1. Go to the "Aadhaar/Other Identities" menu.
  2. Select "Aadhaar Linking".
  3. Enter the District, Zone, Village, and Account Number.
  4. The system will display if your KYC is completed or pending.

How to Find Your Survey Number Without Physical Documents

If you have misplaced your original land papers, you can still identify your Survey Number using the portal's built-in search tools.

1. Search by Name (Pattadar Name)

This method is ideal if you are certain of the name registered in the revenue records:

  • Access the Search: Go to the Adangal or 1-B section of the MeeBhoomi site.
  • Select "Pattadar Name": Instead of inputting a number, select the "Pattadar Name" (పట్టాదారు పేరు) radio button.
  • Refine Your Search: Enter the owner’s name.
  • Expert Tip: Revenue records often use initials or surnames differently. If the full name doesn't work, try searching with just the surname to find a list of all family holdings in that village.
  • Locate Results: A list will populate showing the names of owners along with their associated Survey Numbers and Khata Numbers.

2. Using the "Village Map" (Visual Search)

If text-based searches fail, a visual "Cadastral Map" can help you identify your land based on its physical location:

  • Open Village Maps: Click on the "Village Map" (గ్రామ పటం) icon on the home page.
  • Navigate to Your Area: Input your location details to load the village map.
  • Identify by Landmarks: Use recognizable features like village roads, irrigation canals, or the position of neighboring plots to find your land on the map.
  • Read the Survey ID: Every demarcated boundary box on the map contains a number. This number is your Survey Number.

3. Using the BhuNaksha Portal

The BhuNaksha AP tool provides a sophisticated way to view land records overlaid on satellite imagery:

  • Satellite Overlay: This tool allows you to see actual trees, fences, and buildings on your property via a Google Maps-style view.
  • Coordinate Identification: By clicking on the specific physical area of your land on the satellite map, the system will automatically pull up the official Survey Number and owner details associated with those geographic coordinates.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the difference between Adangal and 1-B?
Adangal provides cultivation and land nature details, while 1-B acts as the legal title deed proving ownership.

Can I use the downloaded Adangal for bank loans?
Yes, the electronically signed Adangal and 1-B records from MeeBhoomi are valid for loan processing and official verification.

How do I fix errors in my land records?
You can file a complaint directly on the portal under the "Complaints" section or visit the nearest MeeSeva center to request a correction.

Is the FMB sketch available for all villages?
FMB sketches are being digitized progressively. If your village map is not available online, you must visit the Mandal Revenue Office (MRO).
